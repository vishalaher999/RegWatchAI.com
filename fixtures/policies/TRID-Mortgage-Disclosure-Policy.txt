SECTION 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 Purpose
This policy establishes First Community Bank's procedures for complying with the TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure (TRID) rule, which implements requirements under the Truth in Lending Act (TILA), the Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act (RESPA), and Regulation Z (12 C.F.R. Part 1026) and Regulation X (12 C.F.R. Part 1024).

1.2 Scope
This policy applies to all closed-end consumer credit transactions secured by real property, including:
- Purchase money mortgages
- Refinance loans
- Construction loans (if applicable)
- Home equity loans (not HELOCs — excluded from TRID)

Excluded transactions:
- Home Equity Lines of Credit (HELOCs) — subject to separate TILA disclosures
- Reverse mortgages — subject to separate TILA requirements
- Loans secured by vacant land (unless construction loan)
- Commercial and investment property loans

SECTION 2: LOAN ESTIMATE REQUIREMENTS

2.1 Triggering Events
A Loan Estimate must be provided after receiving six pieces of information constituting an application:
(1) Consumer's name
(2) Consumer's income
(3) Consumer's Social Security Number (for credit report)
(4) Property address
(5) Estimated value of the property
(6) Loan amount

2.2 Loan Estimate Timing
The Loan Estimate shall be:
- Delivered or placed in mail no later than three business days after receiving an application
- Received by the consumer no later than seven business days before consummation

2.3 Loan Estimate Content
The Loan Estimate shall include:
- General information (loan terms, projected payments, costs at closing)
- Closing cost details (Loan Costs and Other Costs)
- Calculating cash to close
- Comparisons (APR, Total Interest Percentage)
- Other considerations (appraisal, assumption, homeowner's insurance, late payments)
- Contact information

2.4 Good Faith Tolerance Standards
The Bank shall comply with the following tolerance standards:

Zero Tolerance Items (no increase permitted):
- Transfer taxes
- Fees paid to the creditor for required services with no choice
- Third-party fees for services not on approved provider list
- Recording fees

10% Tolerance Items:
- Recording fees (exception when aggregate tolerance applies)
- Third-party fees for required services on approved provider list chosen by consumer
- Prepaid interest, property insurance premiums, initial escrow payments
- Charges for optional third-party services

No Tolerance Items (may change):
- Prepaid interest
- Property insurance premiums
- Initial escrow payment amounts
- Homeowners association dues (paid at closing)

SECTION 3: CLOSING DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS

3.1 Closing Disclosure Timing
The Closing Disclosure shall be:
- Received by the consumer no later than three business days before consummation
- For mailing purposes, presumed received three business days after placing in mail

3.2 Closing Disclosure Content
The Closing Disclosure mirrors the Loan Estimate format and includes final figures for all costs, loan terms, and cash to close.

3.3 Revised Closing Disclosure
A revised Closing Disclosure is required if, before consummation:
- APR becomes inaccurate (exceeds tolerance)
- Loan product changes
- Prepayment penalty is added

A revised Closing Disclosure requires an additional three business day waiting period.

3.4 Changes After Consummation
Post-consummation corrections to Closing Disclosures shall be handled pursuant to Regulation Z § 1026.19(f)(2) procedures.

SECTION 4: REVISED LOAN ESTIMATE

4.1 Permissible Changed Circumstances
A revised Loan Estimate may be issued only upon occurrence of a valid changed circumstance:
- Extraordinary event beyond the Bank's control
- Information specific to the consumer/transaction that was inaccurate or changed
- New information not previously available
- Delay in settlement (under certain conditions)
- Rate locks
- Consumer requests changes to credit terms

4.2 Revised Loan Estimate Timing
A revised Loan Estimate shall be delivered within three business days of receiving information supporting a changed circumstance, and no later than four business days before consummation.

SECTION 5: ESCROW DISCLOSURES AND ACCOUNT MANAGEMENT

5.1 Escrow Disclosure Requirements
For escrow accounts, the Bank shall provide:
- Initial Escrow Account Disclosure Statement at consummation
- Annual Escrow Account Analysis and Statement

5.2 Escrow Account Computation Year
The Bank shall conduct an annual escrow analysis for each escrowed loan to determine:
- Whether a surplus, shortage, or deficiency exists
- The adjusted monthly escrow payment for the coming year

5.3 Surplus, Shortage, Deficiency
- Surplus ≥ $50: Refund within 30 days of analysis
- Shortage: May collect over 12 months if shortage ≤ 1/12 monthly payment; otherwise spread over longer period
- Deficiency: Bank may collect in two months; shortage notice procedures apply

SECTION 6: LOAN OFFICER AND PROCESSOR PROCEDURES

6.1 Loan Origination System Configuration
The Bank's loan origination system (LOS) shall be configured to:
- Automatically generate Loan Estimates upon application receipt
- Calculate and monitor tolerance thresholds
- Generate alerts when revised disclosures may be required
- Track all disclosure delivery dates and methods

6.2 Loan Officer Responsibilities
Loan officers are responsible for:
- Collecting complete application information before triggering disclosures
- Not discouraging applications based on protected characteristics
- Accurately estimating fees and costs on the Loan Estimate
- Documenting changed circumstances before issuing revised Loan Estimates

6.3 Closing/Disclosure Coordinator
The Bank shall designate a TRID compliance coordinator responsible for:
- Reviewing all Closing Disclosures before delivery
- Monitoring consummation dates and waiting periods
- Maintaining a disclosure log for each loan
- Resolving tolerance cure procedures

SECTION 7: TOLERANCE CURE PROCEDURES

7.1 Cure of Good Faith Violations
The Bank may cure a violation of the good faith requirement by refunding to the consumer the amount by which the tolerance was exceeded, at or before consummation or within 60 calendar days after consummation.

7.2 Cure Documentation
All tolerance cures shall be documented, including:
- Original and actual fee amounts
- Cure amount
- Date of refund
- Method of refund

SECTION 8: RECORDKEEPING

8.1 Retention Requirements
The Bank shall retain all TRID-related records for three years from the date of consummation or from the date of disclosures (whichever is longer), including:
- All versions of Loan Estimates and Closing Disclosures
- Evidence of delivery (delivery confirmation, mailing receipts)
- Documentation of changed circumstances for revised Loan Estimates
- Escrow account disclosures and analyses

SECTION 9: TRAINING AND COMPLIANCE TESTING

9.1 Required Training
All mortgage loan officers, processors, closers, and compliance staff shall receive TRID training:
- At hire: within 30 days
- Annual refresher: each calendar year
- Regulatory updates: within 90 days of effective date

9.2 Compliance Testing
The Bank shall conduct quarterly file reviews of closed mortgage loans to test:
- Timely delivery of Loan Estimates and Closing Disclosures
- Accuracy of disclosures
- Appropriate use of revised disclosures
- Tolerance compliance
