SECTION 1: PURPOSE AND SCOPE

1.1 Purpose
This policy establishes First Community Bank's (the "Bank") compliance program under the Bank Secrecy Act (BSA), 31 U.S.C. §§ 5311-5336, and implementing regulations at 31 C.F.R. Chapter X, and the USA PATRIOT Act. The Bank is committed to detecting and reporting financial crimes, including money laundering, terrorist financing, and other illicit activities.

1.2 Scope
This policy applies to all Bank employees, officers, directors, and contractors. All products, services, and customer relationships of the Bank are subject to this policy.

1.3 Regulatory Framework
The Bank's BSA/AML Program is designed to comply with:
- Bank Secrecy Act (31 U.S.C. §§ 5311-5336)
- USA PATRIOT Act (31 U.S.C. § 5318(h))
- FinCEN regulations (31 C.F.R. Chapter X)
- Federal Reserve Regulation H and Y
- FFIEC BSA/AML Examination Manual

SECTION 2: BSA/AML PROGRAM REQUIREMENTS

2.1 Four Pillars of the BSA/AML Program
The Bank maintains a BSA/AML Program based on four pillars:
(a) Policies, procedures, and internal controls
(b) A designated BSA Compliance Officer
(c) Ongoing training for appropriate personnel
(d) Independent testing (audit)

2.2 BSA Compliance Officer
The Bank shall designate a qualified BSA Compliance Officer responsible for:
- Day-to-day oversight of the BSA/AML Program
- Filing required reports with FinCEN
- Ensuring adequate training for all applicable staff
- Reporting to the Board of Directors at least annually

SECTION 3: CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD) AND KNOW YOUR CUSTOMER (KYC)

3.1 Customer Identification Program (CIP)
At account opening, the Bank shall collect and verify:
- Legal name
- Date of birth (for individuals)
- Address
- Identification number (SSN, EIN, or foreign equivalent)

3.2 Customer Due Diligence Requirements
The Bank shall assess the risk level of each customer based on:
- Customer type (individual, business, non-profit)
- Geographic location
- Products and services used
- Expected account activity

3.3 Beneficial Ownership
For legal entity customers, the Bank shall collect beneficial ownership information identifying:
- Each individual who owns 25% or more of the legal entity
- One individual who controls the legal entity

3.4 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)
Higher-risk customers shall be subject to Enhanced Due Diligence including:
- Additional documentation of source of funds
- More frequent review of account activity
- Senior management approval for account opening
- Ongoing monitoring at least annually

SECTION 4: TRANSACTION MONITORING

4.1 Automated Monitoring System
The Bank utilizes an automated transaction monitoring system configured to identify:
- Unusual cash transactions
- Structuring activity
- Rapid movement of funds
- Transactions inconsistent with customer profile

4.2 Currency Transaction Reporting (CTR)
The Bank shall file a Currency Transaction Report (FinCEN Form 104) for:
- Cash transactions exceeding $10,000 in a single business day
- Multiple transactions by or on behalf of the same person exceeding $10,000

4.3 Structuring
The Bank shall not assist any customer in structuring transactions to evade CTR reporting requirements. Detected structuring shall be reported via Suspicious Activity Report.

4.4 Suspicious Activity Reports (SAR)
The Bank shall file a SAR with FinCEN when it knows, suspects, or has reason to suspect that a transaction:
- Involves funds from illegal activity
- Is designed to evade BSA requirements
- Lacks lawful purpose
- Involves a violation of law or regulation
Threshold: $5,000 for known or suspected violations; $2,000 for certain categories

4.5 SAR Confidentiality
The existence of a SAR filing shall not be disclosed to the subject of the report or any other party, except as permitted by law.

SECTION 5: HIGH-RISK CUSTOMERS AND ACTIVITIES

5.1 High-Risk Customer Categories
The following customer types require heightened scrutiny:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their family members
- Foreign financial institutions
- Money services businesses (MSBs)
- Cash-intensive businesses
- Non-profit organizations operating internationally
- Customers in high-risk jurisdictions

5.2 Correspondent Banking
The Bank shall perform enhanced due diligence on foreign correspondent banking relationships including assessment of foreign institution's AML program.

5.3 Private Banking
Private banking accounts shall be subject to Enhanced Due Diligence and senior management oversight.

SECTION 6: OFAC COMPLIANCE

6.1 Screening Requirements
The Bank shall screen all customers, transactions, and counterparties against OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list and applicable sanctions lists.

6.2 Blocked Transactions
Transactions involving sanctioned parties or countries shall be blocked and reported to OFAC as required.

SECTION 7: TRAINING

7.1 Required Training
All Bank employees shall complete BSA/AML training:
- New employees: within 30 days of hire
- All employees: annually thereafter
- BSA-specific staff: more frequent specialized training

7.2 Training Content
Training shall cover:
- Recognition of suspicious activity
- Reporting obligations
- Consequences of non-compliance
- Red flags specific to employee's role

SECTION 8: INDEPENDENT TESTING

8.1 BSA/AML Audit
The Bank shall conduct an independent audit of the BSA/AML Program at least annually. The audit shall assess:
- Adequacy of internal controls
- Accuracy of CTR and SAR filings
- Effectiveness of training
- Quality of transaction monitoring

8.2 Audit Scope
The audit shall be conducted by qualified internal audit staff or an external firm with BSA expertise. Results shall be reported to the Board of Directors and Audit Committee.

SECTION 9: RECORDKEEPING

9.1 Record Retention
The Bank shall maintain BSA-related records for five years from the date of the transaction or account closing, including:
- CIP records
- CTR filings
- SAR filings (maintained confidentially)
- Beneficial ownership certifications
- Wire transfer records

SECTION 10: POLICY VIOLATIONS AND CONSEQUENCES

10.1 Reporting Violations
Employees shall report suspected policy violations to the BSA Compliance Officer or through the Bank's ethics hotline.

10.2 Consequences
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, as well as potential civil and criminal penalties.
